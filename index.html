    Recent Projects

    I am interested in human-computer interaction, especially in education, games, interactive devices and accessibility. I like to work with users and focus on the user experience.

  • Fish for Study

    • Master's degree research program, supervised by Dr. Gail Hopkins. Fish for Study hopes to provide a digital intervention software that uses gamification to provide motivation in order to achieve the goal of avoiding distractions from mobile devices for learners.

  • MaskMe

    • Supervised by Qin Wu, MaskMe was a wearable mask that attempted to achieve eye contact intervention for children with autism through wearable computing. A large number of user research methods were used in the project, which was developed by Arduino and finally evaluated by field experiments.

  • E-CASE

    • Bachelor's degree research program, supervised by Qin Wu. E-case was an environmental protection education projection game designed for senior primary school students in China. The project acquired requirements and conducted qualitative analysis through questionnaire and interview methods, and developed and implemented the project using OpenCV-Python, Unity, and Arduino. After the development was completed, elementary school students were invited to conduct laboratory experiments to evaluate it.

  • CheChe

    • CheChe was a group of tracking robots developed using OpenCV-Python and Arduino. This project was supervised by Wu Qin.CheChe has been accepted to the UIST 2019 Innovation Competition.
